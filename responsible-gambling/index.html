Signs of Problem Gambling

Recognizing problematic gambling behavior early is crucial for maintaining control. Canadian players should be aware of these warning signs in themselves or others:

Behavioral Signs Financial Signs Emotional Signs
Spending more time or money than intended on Chicken Road Borrowing money to continue gambling Feeling restless or irritable when not gambling
Chasing losses by increasing bets frequently Neglecting bills or essential expenses Gambling to escape problems or negative feelings
Lying to family about gambling activities Selling personal items to fund gambling Experiencing guilt or shame after gambling sessions
Neglecting work, family, or social responsibilities Using savings meant for other purposes Mood swings related to winning or losing

If you recognize several of these signs in your own behavior, we encourage you to use Chicken Road's responsible gaming tools or contact Canadian support services immediately.

Self-Exclusion & Limits

Chicken Road provides Canadian players with powerful tools to manage their gaming activity. These features are easily accessible through your account settings and can be customized to suit your personal circumstances.

  • Deposit Limits: Set daily, weekly, or monthly maximum deposit amounts that cannot be exceeded
  • Time Limits: Receive reminders or automatic logouts after predetermined gaming sessions
  • Reality Checks: Regular notifications about time spent and money wagered on Chicken Road
  • Temporary Time-Outs: Take breaks from gaming for 24 hours up to 6 weeks
  • Self-Exclusion: Complete break from Chicken Road for periods from 6 months to 5 years

Canadian players can implement these controls immediately through their Chicken Road account dashboard. Once set, cooling-off and self-exclusion periods cannot be reversed early, ensuring you maintain your commitment to responsible gaming.

FAQ on Responsible Gambling

What is the legal gambling age for Chicken Road in Canada?
The legal age varies by province: 18 in Alberta, Manitoba, and Quebec; 19 in all other provinces and territories. Chicken Road verifies age during registration to ensure compliance with Canadian regulations.

How can I set deposit limits on my Chicken Road account?
Canadian players can set deposit limits through their account settings. These limits take effect immediately and cannot be increased during the current period, helping you maintain control over your spending.

What should I do if I think I have a gambling problem?
First, use Chicken Road's self-exclusion tools to prevent further play. Then contact a Canadian problem gambling helpline for professional support. Remember that acknowledging the issue is an important first step toward recovery.

Are there resources for family members concerned about a loved one's gambling?
Yes, many Canadian organizations offer support for affected family members. The Canadian Problem Gambling Helpline (1-800-463-1554) can direct you to appropriate resources in your province.

How does Chicken Road promote responsible gambling?
Beyond the tools mentioned above, Chicken Road incorporates responsible gambling messages throughout our platform, provides regular reality checks to Canadian players, and trains our staff to identify and assist players who may be developing problematic behaviors.

Can I permanently close my Chicken Road account?
Yes, Canadian players can request permanent account closure through our customer support team. This decision is irreversible, so we recommend trying a temporary self-exclusion period first if you're uncertain.
